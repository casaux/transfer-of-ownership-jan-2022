Title

Registration of drones/remotely piloted aircraft in Australia

Drone usage is the fastest growing sector in Australian aviation. Registration helps CASA ensure the safety of the general public and other aviation sectors.

When do you need to register a drone?

You must register your drone or remotely piloted aircraft in Australia if any of the following apply:

  • You are operating your drone outdoors and your drone is over 250g in weight
  • Your are not operating at a site under the control of the Model Aeronautical Association of Australia (MAAA) or Australian Miniature Aerosports Society (AMAS)
  • You are operating your drone outdoors (or indoors but for commercial reasons)

How do I register my drone?

  1. Create a myCASA account and verify your identity online. You can now do this using myGovID see below for further information
  2. Apply for or link your ARN
  3. Provide drone details (weight, manufacturer and serial number)
  4. Pay for your drone registration

Take the first step now by creating your free myCASA account or if you already have an account, log in and continue the steps.

Register your drone in myCASA

What is Digital Identity?

myGovID is your Digital Identity and makes it easier to prove who you are online. It's like the 100 point ID check, but on your smart device. Your Digital Identity makes accessing government services online simpler, safer and more secure.

What if I don't register my drone?

Anyone operating a drone that has not been registered and is operating under the conditions described above risks being fined.

  • Advisory Circulars for drone operations
  • CASA 96/17 - Direction — operation of certain unmanned aircraft
