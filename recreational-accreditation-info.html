What is recreational operator accreditation?

Model aircraft (drone weighing more than 250g) operators do not need to hold a remote pilot licence or commercial RPA accreditiation to operate model aircraft for sport and recreation. An accreditiation is an authorisation similiar to a licence, but there are certain rules around the where, when and how these model aircraft can be flown. When you hold a recreational operator accreditation you may operate:

  • model aircraft (drone weighing more than 250g) for sport or recreation

If you're operating model aircraft, you're required to:
  • get accredited to show that you understand the general drone safety rules and standard operating conditions
  • register your model aircraft with CASA

You can read more about the general rules and the standard operating conditions that apply to recreational operations on our website. We've also developed a plain English guide to help you understand the rules that apply to recreational aperations.

Accreditiation outcomes

If you pass you will receive an accreditiation for the following RPA:

  • model aircraft (drone weighing more than 250g) for sport or recreation

This accreditation will also allow you to:

  • supervise persons under 16 years to operate a drone (model aircraft) provided you are 18 years or older
