What is Commercial RPA operator accreditation?

Micro RPA and excluded RPA operators do not need to hold a remote pilot licence to operate certain RPA for activities other than sport and recreation. An accreditiation is an authorisation similiar to a licence, but there are certain rules around the size of the RPA, and where, when and how these RPA can be flown. When you hold a Commercial RPA operator accreditation you may operate:

  • Micro RPA (RPA that weigh 250g or less), for activities other than sport and recreation.
  • Excluded RPA:
    • RPA that weigh more than 250g, up to 2kg for activities other than sport and recreation
    RPA that weigh more than 250g, up to 25kg, operated only over land owned or occupied by the RPA owner for certain operations.

If you're operating excluded RPA, or micro RPA, you're required to:
  • get accredited to show that you understand the general drone safety rules and standard operating conditions
  • register your RPA with CASA

You can read more about the general rules and the standard operating conditions that apply to micro and excluded RPA operations on our website. We've also developed a plain English guide to help you understand the rules that apply to micro and excluded RPA.

Accreditiation outcomes

If you pass you will receive an accreditiation for the following RPA:

  • micro RPA:
    • weighing 250g or less for activities other than sport or recreation
  • excluded RPA
    • weighing more than 250g up to 2kg, for activities other than sport or recreation
    weighing more than 250g up to 25kg, operated only over land owned or occupied by the RPA owner for certain operations.

This accreditation will also allow you to:

  • supervise persons under 16 years to operate a drone (micro RPA, excluded RPA or model aircraft) provided you are 18 years or older
  • operarte a model aircraft (drone weighing more than 250g) for sport or recreation
